What is a "stain"?

A "stain" is a binary spot in an image, where white pixels represent the spot and black pixels the background. It is used in image analysis to estimate areas of interest.

How does the calculation work?

n random points are generated within the image. The number of points that fall inside the white spot is counted.

How is the area estimated?

The estimated area is:
Area = (Total area) × (points inside / n)
Where "Total area" is the width × height of the image, "points inside" is the number of points that fall inside the spot, and "n" is the total number of generated points.

What does the result mean?

The result is an estimate of the spot area in square pixels. You can compare different images or point configurations. If you repeat the process, you will see that the result varies slightly due to randomness, but stabilizes with more points.
